        MICHAEL
        HANSEN

        Michael is one of the original founders of Budo Martial Arts and a 1st degree black belt in Brazilian Jiu-jitsu under Robert Drysdale.

        He has trained in both Brazilian Jiu-jitsu and Muay Thai Kickboxing since 2009, competing at the IBJJF World Masters, Pan American championships, and is a 2014 North American Grappling Association (NAGA) champion.

        He has been coaching for over 12 years, as well as training alongside some of the biggest names in Jiu-jitsu include Ricardo De La Riva, Rubens “Cobrinha” Charles, and Roberto “Cyborg” Abreu. Michael has stood atop the podium numerous times, including the 2014 NAGA Portland championship, and two 2019 black belt titles – the IBJJF Seattle Open Masters featherweight championship and the IBJJF Portland Open featherweight championship.

        His extensive experience and leadership will surely aid the competitive team at Budo, help the recreational members attain their martial arts goals, and provide a safe, fun and productive space in the kids Jiu-jitsu & Kickboxing classes.
